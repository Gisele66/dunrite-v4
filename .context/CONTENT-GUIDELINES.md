# Dunrite Dave – Content Guidelines

## Overview

This document defines the tone, voice, messaging rules, and content strategy for the Dunrite Dave website.

**Purpose:** Maintain consistent brand voice, appropriate positioning, and effective messaging across all pages and updates.

---

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Core Voice Attributes

**Professional but approachable**
- Use clear, direct language
- Avoid jargon or overly technical terms
- Sound like a trusted local expert, not a corporate entity

**Confident and capable**
- Emphasize experience and reliability
- Speak with authority about difficult routes and services
- Don't oversell or use excessive superlatives

**Down-to-earth**
- Write like you're talking to a neighbor
- No corporate buzzwords or marketing fluff
- Be honest about what you do and where you go

**Service-focused**
- Lead with customer benefit, not company ego
- Emphasize care, reliability, and accountability
- Show, don't just tell (use specifics)

## Content Patterns to Follow

### Headlines

**Do:**
- Lead with benefit or capability
- Use action words (Moving, Delivery, Washing)
- Be specific about location/service
- Keep under 10 words when possible

**Examples:**
✅ "Moving, delivery & washing — done right."  
✅ "From Prince George to Haida Gwaii — and everywhere between"  
✅ "A name people in the North trust"

**Don't:**
❌ "We're the best moving company in BC!"  
❌ "Revolutionary moving solutions"  
❌ "Your premier logistics partner"

### Body Copy

**Do:**
- Start sentences with subject (We, You, The team)
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Include specific details (routes, years, numbers)
- Use "we" not "our company" or "Dunrite" in third person

**Example:**
✅ "We've been running these routes since 1993. When you call, you talk to Michelle or Dave directly—the same people who estimate, schedule, and often drive. Your move isn't handed off to strangers."

**Don't:**
❌ "Dunrite is committed to providing industry-leading moving solutions through innovative processes and customer-centric paradigms."

### Lists

**Do:**
- Use parallel structure
- Lead with action or benefit
- Keep items under 10 words
- Use check icons for benefits, pin icons for locations

**Example:**
✅ 
- Owner-operated since 1993 — accountable, hands-on service
- Specialists in remote & long-haul routes others won't take
- Fully equipped fleet and trained, careful crews

## Franchise Messaging Rules

### Golden Rule
**Never make franchising the primary message or main navigation item.**

### Allowed Mentions

**Homepage:**
- Small section near bottom (current placement is correct)
- Soft badge like "Now Growing"
- Focus on "opportunity" not "recruitment"

**Dedicated Franchise Page:**
- More detail allowed here
- Still professional, not salesy
- Emphasize proven model, not "get rich quick"

**Footer:**
- Small link is fine
- No promotional language

### Forbidden

❌ "Franchise opportunities available!" in hero  
❌ Franchise as top navigation item  
❌ Pop-ups or aggressive franchise CTAs  
❌ Franchise content above the fold on homepage  
❌ Making it feel like an MLM or network marketing

### Tone for Franchise Content

**Do:**
✅ "We're exploring partnerships with operators who share our standards."  
✅ "A proven, profitable model serving difficult markets."  
✅ "If you're interested, let's talk."

**Don't:**
❌ "Join the Dunrite family TODAY!"  
❌ "Limited territories available—act now!"  
❌ "Be your own boss with our proven system!"

## Content Update Rules

### Before Changing Content

1. Read this guideline first
2. Check if change fits brand voice
3. Maintain messaging hierarchy
4. Preserve working copy unless there's a clear improvement
5. Test tone against voice attributes

### What Can Change Freely

✅ Specific route examples  
✅ Service details (if accurate)  
✅ Team member updates  
✅ Contact information  
✅ Hours/availability  
✅ Pricing approach (if client approves)

### What Needs Approval

⚠️ Major headline changes  
⚠️ Repositioning services (primary to secondary)  
⚠️ Franchise messaging adjustments  
⚠️ Brand tagline changes  
⚠️ Company history/timeline

### What Should Not Change

🛑 Core positioning (family-owned, since 1993, route expertise)  
🛑 Service offerings (without client confirmation)  
🛑 Contact information (without client providing update)  
🛑 Tagline: "If you want it done, have it Dunrite"
